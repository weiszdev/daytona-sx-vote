Track Notes

Ford Field has hosted supercross since 2006 and typically delivers one of the most packed stadiums on the schedule. The enclosed dome means zero weather concerns—just pure racing conditions under the lights. Detroit has historically rewarded aggressive riders who can handle tight, technical layouts. The dirt tends to break down quickly on the NFL turf base, creating deep ruts and braking bumps by the time the mains roll around.

Chase Sexton makes his return here tonight after three rounds out. This is also the halfway point of the 250SX East series, making it a pivotal round for Cole Davies to defend his six-point lead over Seth Hammaker. With Haiden Deegan still fuming from last week's penalty, expect fireworks in the quarter-liter class.

This Week in Moto
Top Stories
⚡ 250SX
Deegan Robbed Again
Haiden Deegan crossed the line four seconds clear at Birmingham—only to catch a penalty for clipping white track markers on a lane switch. Cole Davies inherits the win. That's twice this season Deegan has lost a victory on a ruling. His response? "Everyone knows who won."
⚖ 450SX
Sexton Returns Tonight
Three rounds on the sideline with a hip and lower-back injury from a February practice crash. Chase Sexton lines back up at Ford Field tonight sitting seventh in points. The former champion has just one podium so far this season—a win at Anaheim 2. Detroit is his shot to claw back into the title picture.
🔥 450SX
Roczen: 12th to 2nd
A buried start didn't slow Ken Roczen down. He ripped through the Birmingham pack from 12th to second, picking off 10 riders along the way. Combined with his fifth heat win of the year, Roczen is firmly back in the championship conversation sitting third at 190 points.
🏆 450SX
Lawrence Tightens Grip
Hunter Lawrence took the Birmingham main and stretched his lead to nine points over Eli Tomac, who crashed in his heat and had to fight through the LCQ. With seven rounds left, Lawrence's consistency is building a wall—but nine points is nothing in supercross.
🩹 Injury Report
Triumph's Double Blow
Austin Forkner broke his hand during Houston press day and needs surgery—done for supercross. Then Jalek Swoll tore his Achilles in Birmingham qualifying. Two factory riders down in the span of a week. Gage Linville has been called up to fill the 250SX seat for the rest of the season.
⏱ Season Watch
Jett Still Sidelined
Jett Lawrence's fractured talus and navicular from a December training crash continue to keep him off the bike. No firm return date has been set. The defending champion was the pre-season favorite, and his absence has completely reshaped the 450 title fight. Every week without Jett is another week the championship slips further away.

Bold Predictions — Detroit
Sexton Podiums in His Comeback
Three rounds off gave Sexton time to heal properly. He won Anaheim 2 before the injury, proving he has top-level speed this season. Ford Field's tight layout rewards technique over raw fitness—and technique doesn't rust in three weeks.
Roczen Holeshots & Checks Out
Ken ripped from 12th to 2nd at Birmingham. Imagine what happens when he doesn't have to dig out of a first-turn pileup. He's won five heats this year—the starts are there. If he grabs the main event holeshot, nobody is catching him.
Deegan Goes Full Send for Payback
After getting a win stripped last week on a track-marker penalty, Deegan is racing angry. That can go one of two ways—but when he channels that energy right, he's uncatchable in the 250 class. Expect him to leave zero doubt at the stripe tonight.
Tomac Closes the Gap to Single Digits
Eli crashed in his Birmingham heat and still salvaged a podium through the LCQ. That's championship DNA. He's been the fastest rider on raw speed all season—he just needs a clean main event. Detroit's historically been good to Tomac. The 9-point deficit shrinks tonight.
